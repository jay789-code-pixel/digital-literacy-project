# 🎣 Phishing — Cybercrime Case Study




## 📌 What is Phishing?

Phishing is when a **cybercriminal pretends to be someone you trust** — like your bank, Google, or even your boss — to trick you into giving away your password, OTP, or personal details.


---

## 📖 Case Study — The Fake Bank Email

> *This is a realistic fictional scenario for educational purposes.*

**Ravi**, a 34-year-old salaried employee from Pune, received an email one morning that looked exactly like it was from his bank — same logo, same colors, even the same "From" name.

The email said:

> *"Your account has been temporarily locked due to suspicious activity. Click here to verify your details within 24 hours or your account will be permanently suspended."*

Ravi panicked. He clicked the link, which opened a website that **looked identical** to his bank's real site. He entered his account number, password, and OTP.

Within minutes, ₹87,000 was transferred out of his account.

---

# 🔄 How It Happens — Step by Step

## What the Attacker Does 

1.  Creates a **fake email** that looks real (spoofed sender address) 
2.  Writes a **scary or urgent message** — "Your account is locked!" 
3.  Adds a **fake link** that leads to a cloned website 
4.  Victim enters their **login details or OTP** on the fake site 
5.  Attacker **steals the data** and uses it immediately 

---

## 🎯 Who Gets Targeted?

- Working professionals who use net banking or UPI
- Elderly people who are not very tech-savvy
- Students applying for scholarships or jobs online
- Anyone who shops on e-commerce platforms
- People who recently made a large transaction (easy to scare)

---

## ⚠️ Consequences

**For the Victim:**
- Financial loss (sometimes entire savings)
- Stolen identity — used to open fake loans or cards
- Mental stress and feeling of shame
- Difficult and slow process to recover money

**Legal side:**
- Phishing is a punishable offence under **Section 66C and 66D of the IT Act, 2000** in India
- Attackers can face **3 years of imprisonment** and heavy fines

---

## 🛡️ How to Stay Safe

- **Never click** links in emails/SMS asking for urgent action
- Always **check the URL** before entering any password
- Banks **never ask for OTP** over email or phone
- Enable **2-factor authentication** on all accounts
- When in doubt — **go directly to the official website**, don't use the link



*"Think before you click. One wrong tap can cost you everything."* 🔐
